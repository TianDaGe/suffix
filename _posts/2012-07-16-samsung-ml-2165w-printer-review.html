--- layout: post title: Samsung ML-2165W printer review date: 2012-07-16 16:20:11 updated: 2012-08-06 22:56:51 ---

A few weeks ago my old and trusty HP Deskjet 3845 Color Inkjet printer died while printing some enveloppes. I don't even remember when I bought this printer, probably over 10 years ago. I happened to pass an electronics shop so went in and got me a Samsung ML-2165W for €59.45. An impulsive buy.

Black & white laser printer

I had no idea what to buy but I did know I wanted a black & white laser printer. I don't print a lot, maybe 5 pages a month. Ink evaporates and cartridges are expensive. Same goes for color. As I never print photos (online print services are so cheap these days) the color ink was only used to print advertising on plane or train tickets or the occasional website. So I figured a black & white printer would save me from checking the printer settings each time.

Bye, bye HP

My previous printer served me well, why wouldn't I opt for an HP one again? The most important reason is probably their software, a driver is all I need, please don't include tons of other applications I don't need. It gives me the impression I have to pay for something I don't want. I buy a printer, not a photo manager. On top of that HP got some bad press lately. I assume they loose money on their hardware and make a ton of profit on the cartridges. Maybe all printer vendors do?

ML-2165W and USB

Unpacking the Samsung ML-2165W. It has everything in the box, a power and USB cable, nice. I have seen printers where you had to buy a ‘printer cable’ (= USB cable) separately in the past. Connected the printer via USB and printed a test page. Worked out of the box. The laser printer is fast (compared with the old inkjet) and quality is what I expect from a €60 printer. Not bad, not great, but certainly good enough for printing text and plane tickets. So far, so good.

Wireless printing

The Samsung ML-2165W is also a wireless printer. Compatible with Mac OS X. Or so they say.

Installed the Samsung Easy Printer Manager on Mac OS X Lion to configure the wireless settings on the printer as I don't have a router that supports WPS (well, I do, but that's another story). The Easy Printer Manager is as unpredictable as I had feared. The printer, still connected via USB, didn't show up in the Easy Printer Manager. Sometimes it did, only to disappear again. The wizard did not find the printer and blamed me for not connecting the USB cable. If the printer did show up it would sometimes tell me this model did not support WIFI. When I reboot it enough I may be able to access the WIFI settings menu. The menu does not show any wireless networks or something that looks like the settings one would expect for a wireless device. A few demotivating hours later I gave up and booted the Windows VM. Ran the installation wizard and finished the setup process in 5 minutes.

Samsung, don't include Mac OS X software if you don't care about it. Don't include a support link on your website if it only refreshes the current page. Don't show 4 out of 5 star ratings if you don't show the actual reviews and almost every other review on the internet makes you look bad. Samsung, please care about your clients.

Once past the initial setup (thanks to Windows) it works great. All the Macs in the house pick up the printer and fetch and install the driver automatically.

Conclusion

A great little printer for little money (still have to see what a new toner will cost me). It's fast and the quality is what I expect from it. Could it be improved? Sure, fixing the Mac OS X software (or leaving it out and providing a web interface) would go a long way. Having some minimal customer support or a FAQ page might have made me a happy customer.
